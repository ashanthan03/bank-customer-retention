EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
─────────────────

This research evaluates customer retention through the lens of behavioral engagement
and product utilization rather than traditional demographic indicators. Analysis of
10,000 European bank customers reveals critical insights:

- Active members show 12.58% higher retention than inactive members (85.73% vs 73.15%)
- Multi-product customers achieve significantly lower churn than single-product customers
- Premium inactive customers represent a high risk segment with 30.47% churn rate
- Relationship Strength Index (RSI) provides reliable churn prediction (4.92pt gap)
- 2-product customers churn at only 7.58% — the optimal product count for retention

Key Finding: Engagement and product adoption drive retention MORE than
demographics or financial metrics alone.


1. INTRODUCTION & PROBLEM STATEMENT
─────────────────────────────────────

1.1 Banking Industry Context

Customer churn remains a critical challenge for European banks. Traditional
approaches focus on demographics (age, income, geography) and financial metrics
(balance, salary). However, emerging evidence suggests that customer behavior —
specifically engagement and product utilization — are stronger predictors of retention.

1.2 Research Gap

Despite access to engagement and product data, banks often lack:

a) Quantitative insight into which behaviors drive retention
b) Clarity on whether product depth reduces churn
c) Evidence on whether high balances alone ensure loyalty

As a result, retention strategies remain generic and misaligned with actual
customer behavior patterns.

1.3 Research Objectives

PRIMARY OBJECTIVES:
1. Evaluate the relationship between engagement and churn
2. Measure retention impact of product count and product mix
3. Identify disengaged yet high-value customers (at-risk segment)

SECONDARY OBJECTIVES:
4. Support engagement-driven retention strategies
5. Improve product bundling decisions
6. Reduce silent churn among premium customers


2. METHODOLOGY & DATA FRAMEWORK
─────────────────────────────────

2.1 Dataset Description

Source: European Central Bank Customer Database
Sample Size: 10,000 active and inactive customers
Geographic Coverage: France (5,014), Germany (2,509), Spain (2,477)
Time Period: Historical snapshot (cross-sectional)

Key Variables:
- CustomerId: Unique identifier
- CreditScore: Credit worthiness assessment (range: 350-850)
- Geography: France, Spain, or Germany
- Gender: Male/Female demographic
- Age: Customer age (18-92 years)
- Tenure: Duration with bank (0-10 years)
- Balance: Account balance in EUR (0 to 250,898)
- NumOfProducts: Count of bank products (1-4 products)
- HasCrCard: Binary indicator of credit card ownership
- IsActiveMember: Binary engagement indicator (0=Inactive, 1=Active)
- EstimatedSalary: Annual salary estimate
- Exited: CHURN TARGET (0=Retained, 1=Churned)

2.2 Data Quality & Validation

- No missing values detected across all 14 columns
- Binary variables validated (IsActiveMember, HasCrCard, Exited: 0/1 only)
- Demographic outliers reviewed and retained
- Balance distribution confirmed (25th percentile = 0, median = 97,198)
- Churn labeling accuracy validated (binary, 20.37% overall churn rate)


3. ANALYTICAL FRAMEWORK
────────────────────────

3.1 Engagement Classification

Customers segmented into four behavioral profiles:

PROFILE 1: Active Engaged (Multi-Product, Active)
- Characteristics: 2+ products, high engagement
- Count: 2,588 customers
- Churn Rate: 9.66%
- Strategic Value: CORE REVENUE SEGMENT

PROFILE 2: Active Low-Product (Single Product, Active)
- Characteristics: 1 product, high engagement
- Count: 2,563 customers
- Churn Rate: 18.92%
- Strategic Value: CROSS-SELL OPPORTUNITY

PROFILE 3: Inactive Premium (Low Engagement, High Balance)
- Characteristics: Inactive, high account balance (above 75th percentile)
- Count: 1,247 customers
- Churn Rate: 30.47%
- Strategic Value: AT-RISK PREMIUM SEGMENT

PROFILE 4: Inactive Disengaged (Low Engagement, Low Activity)
- Characteristics: Inactive, minimal product use
- Count: 3,602 customers
- Churn Rate: 25.60%
- Strategic Value: RECOVERY CHALLENGE

Key Insight: Engagement status overrides financial metrics in predicting churn.


3.2 Product Utilization Analysis

Hypothesis: Product adoption creates switching costs and reduces churn.

Findings:

  Product Count    Customers    Churned    Churn Rate    Retention %
  1 Product        5,084        1,409      27.71%        72.29%
  2 Products       4,590          348       7.58%        92.42%
  3 Products         266          220      82.71%        17.29%
  4 Products          60           60     100.00%         0.00%

Critical Findings:
- 2-product customers are the most loyal (92.42% retention)
- 3 and 4-product customers show anomalously high churn — indicating over-selling risk
- Optimal product count for retention is exactly 2 products

Product Depth Index: 1.21x
Interpretation: Multi-product customers are 21% more likely to remain loyal.


3.3 Engagement Impact Assessment

Hypothesis: Member activity level is the primary behavioral driver of retention.

Findings:

  Member Status    Customers    Churned    Retention %    Churn Rate
  Active           5,151          736      85.73%         14.27%
  Inactive         4,849        1,301      73.15%         26.85%

Engagement Retention Ratio: 1.17x
Interpretation: Active members are 17% more likely to retain than inactive members.

Impact Quantification:
If 50% of inactive members are activated (2,425 customers):
- Potential churn reduction: approximately 320 retained customers
- Retention rate improvement: +6.4 percentage points

3.5 Credit Card Stickiness

  Credit Card      Customers    Churned    Retention %

Finding: Credit card ownership has minimal impact on retention in this dataset

3.6 Relationship Strength Index (RSI)

Composite metric combining 5 engagement dimensions:

RSI = (Active Status x 25) + (Products/4 x 25) + (Tenure/10 x 25)
      + (Credit Card x 15) + (Balance/Max Balance x 10)

Scale: 0 to 100

  Status        Mean RSI    Risk Level
  Retained      49.61       Healthy
  Churned       44.69       At-Risk

RSI Gap: 4.92 points (11% higher for retained customers)

Sticky Customer Profiles:

  Profile                  Count    Churn Rate    Avg RSI    Avg Balance
  Sticky Elite             1,565    11.82%        69.93      $65,295
  Sticky Multi-Product     1,023     6.35%        52.64      $34,372
  Sticky Active            2,012    17.59%        61.86     $102,316
  At-Risk Premium          1,247    30.47%        38.19     $148,858
  High Churn Risk          4,153    25.36%        36.28      $56,832

Key Finding: Sticky Multi-Product customers have the lowest churn at 6.35%
despite lower average balance — proving engagement beats wealth.

5. STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS
──────────────────────────────

RECOMMENDATION 1: ENGAGEMENT-FIRST RETENTION STRATEGY
Priority: Highest
Current State: 4,849 customers inactive (48.49%)
Target State: Below 30% inactive

Actions:
- Launch automated SMS/email campaigns for inactive accounts
- Assign relationship managers to premium inactive customers
- Introduce mobile app usage incentives and transaction rewards
Expected Impact: 320+ churns prevented per campaign cycle


RECOMMENDATION 2: OPTIMAL PRODUCT BUNDLING — TARGET 2 PRODUCTS
Priority: Highest
Current State: 5,084 single-product customers (50.84%)
Target State: Move customers to exactly 2 products — avoid over-selling to 3 or 4

Actions:
- Identify which 2-product combinations produce the lowest churn
- Flag 326 customers with 3-4 products for urgent review
- Adjust sales team incentives to reward quality bundling over quantity
Expected Impact: Reduce single-product churn by 20 percentage points


RECOMMENDATION 3: AT-RISK PREMIUM CUSTOMER RECOVERY
Priority: Critical
Current State: 1,247 at-risk premium customers, $185.6M AUM
Target State: Reduce churn below 20% (currently 30.47%)

Actions:
- Week 1: Personal outreach call from relationship manager
- Offer wealth management and investment advisory services
- Provide priority customer treatment and dedicated support line
Expected AUM Protected: $185,625,527 over 90-day intervention


RECOMMENDATION 4: RSI MONITORING SYSTEM
Priority: High

Actions:
- Calculate RSI monthly for all customers
- Automated alerts for customers with RSI below 40
- Immediate contact for RSI dropping more than 5 points in a month
Expected Impact: Proactive early warning system to prevent churn before it happens


RECOMMENDATION 5: ZERO-BALANCE CUSTOMER ACTIVATION
Priority: Medium
Current State: 3,617 customers with zero balance (36.17%)

Actions:
- Promote savings accounts and salary account migration
- Target 3,909 high-salary, low-balance customers with investment products
Expected Impact: Increase AUM and deepen customer relationships

7. LIMITATIONS & FUTURE RESEARCH
──────────────────────────────────

7.1 Study Limitations

- Cross-sectional design — no temporal trends observed
- Limited to 3 geographies (France, Spain, Germany)
- No transaction-level behavioral data available
- No external macro factors considered (interest rates, competitor actions)
- Churn definition: simple binary exit — reasons not captured
- 3 and 4-product anomaly requires further investigation

7.2 Future Research Directions

1. Time-Series Analysis — track churn predictive windows (3-6 months before exit)
2. Product Interaction Analysis — identify which 2-product combos have lowest churn
3. Predictive Modeling — Random Forest / XGBoost churn prediction
4. Segmentation Refinement — K-means clustering for micro-segment interventions
5. Zero-Balance Investigation — understand why 36% hold zero balance


8. CONCLUSION
──────────────

This analysis fundamentally reframes customer churn from a demographic perspective
to a behavioral and relationship-strength lens. Four critical findings emerge:

1. ENGAGEMENT MATTERS
   Active members retain at 85.73% vs inactive at 73.15% (1.17x ratio).
   With 4,849 inactive customers, engagement activation is the top priority.

2. TWO PRODUCTS IS THE SWEET SPOT
   2-product customers churn at only 7.58% vs 27.71% for single-product.
   But 3+ products causes churn to spike — over-selling must stop.

3. AT-RISK PREMIUM SEGMENT DEMANDS IMMEDIATE ACTION
   1,247 high-balance inactive customers hold $185.6M AUM.
   At 30.47% churn rate, approximately $56.5M is at immediate risk.

4. ZERO-BALANCE CUSTOMERS ARE AN UNTAPPED OPPORTUNITY
   3,617 customers (36%) hold zero balance yet remain with the bank.
   These represent significant cross-sell and deposit-growth opportunities.

By implementing the five recommendations, the bank can protect $185.6M in
at-risk AUM, reduce single-product churn by up to 20 percentage points, and
transform from reactive to data-driven retention strategy.

The evidence is clear: Customer retention is not about balance sheets — it is
about relationships, meaningful engagement, and optimal product integration.
