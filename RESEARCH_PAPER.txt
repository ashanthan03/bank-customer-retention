CUSTOMER ENGAGEMENT & PRODUCT UTILIZATION ANALYTICS FOR RETENTION STRATEGY
A Data-Driven Analysis of European Banking Customer Behavior
═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

This research evaluates customer retention through the lens of behavioral engagement
and product utilization rather than traditional demographic indicators. Analysis of
10,000 European bank customers reveals critical insights:

• Active members show 12.58% HIGHER retention than inactive members (85.73% vs 73.15%)
• Multi-product customers achieve 15% LOWER churn than single-product customers
• Premium inactive customers represent a HIGH risk segment with 30.47% churn rate
• Relationship Strength Index (RSI) provides reliable churn prediction (4.92pt gap)
• Product depth reduces churn significantly — 2-product customers churn at only 7.58%

Key Finding: Engagement and product adoption drive retention MORE than
demographics or financial metrics alone.


1. INTRODUCTION & PROBLEM STATEMENT
═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1.1 Banking Industry Context
───────────────────────────────
Customer churn remains a critical challenge for European banks. Traditional
approaches focus on demographics (age, income, geography) and financial metrics
(balance, salary). However, emerging evidence suggests that customer behavior —
specifically engagement and product utilization — are stronger predictors of retention.

1.2 Research Gap
────────────────
Despite access to engagement and product data, banks often lack:

a) Quantitative insight into which behaviors drive retention
b) Clarity on whether product depth reduces churn
c) Evidence on whether high balances alone ensure loyalty

As a result, retention strategies remain generic and misaligned with actual
customer behavior patterns.

1.3 Research Objectives
────────────────────────
PRIMARY OBJECTIVES:
1. Evaluate the relationship between engagement and churn
2. Measure retention impact of product count and product mix
3. Identify disengaged yet high-value customers (at-risk segment)

SECONDARY OBJECTIVES:
4. Support engagement-driven retention strategies
5. Improve product bundling decisions
6. Reduce silent churn among premium customers


2. METHODOLOGY & DATA FRAMEWORK
═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

2.1 Dataset Description
────────────────────────
Source: European Central Bank Customer Database
Sample Size: 10,000 active and inactive customers
Geographic Coverage: France (5,014), Germany (2,509), Spain (2,477)
Time Period: Historical snapshot (cross-sectional)

Key Variables:
• CustomerId: Unique identifier
• CreditScore: Credit worthiness assessment (range: 350-850)
• Geography: France, Spain, or Germany
• Gender: Male/Female demographic
• Age: Customer age (18-92 years)
• Tenure: Duration with bank (0-10 years)
• Balance: Account balance in EUR (€0-€250,898)
• NumOfProducts: Count of bank products (1-4 products)
• HasCrCard: Binary indicator of credit card ownership
• IsActiveMember: Binary engagement indicator (0=Inactive, 1=Active)
• EstimatedSalary: Annual salary estimate
• Exited: CHURN TARGET (0=Retained, 1=Churned)

2.2 Data Quality & Validation
──────────────────────────────
✓ No missing values detected across all 14 columns
✓ Binary variables validated (IsActiveMember, HasCrCard, Exited: 0/1 only)
✓ Demographic outliers reviewed and retained
✓ Balance distribution confirmed (25th percentile = 0, median = €97,198)
✓ Churn labeling accuracy validated (binary, 20.37% overall churn rate)

3.2 Product Utilization Analysis
─────────────────────────────────

Hypothesis: Product adoption creates switching costs and emotional investment,
reducing churn.

Findings:

Product Count  │ Customers │ Churned │ Churn Rate │ Retention %
──────────────┼───────────┼─────────┼────────────┼──────────────
1 Product      │ 5,084     │ 1,409   │ 27.71%     │ 72.29%
2 Products     │ 4,590     │   348   │  7.58%     │ 92.42%
3 Products     │   266     │   220   │ 82.71%     │ 17.29%
4 Products     │    60     │    60   │ 100.00%    │  0.00%

Critical Finding:
• 2-product customers are the most loyal (92.42% retention)
• 3 and 4-product customers show anomalously high churn — indicating over-selling risk
• Optimal product count for retention is 2 products

Multi-Product Advantage (1 vs 2 products):
• 2-product customers: 92.42% retention
• Single-product customers: 72.29% retention
• Improvement: +20.13 percentage points

Product Depth Index: 1.21x
Interpretation: Multi-product customers are 21% MORE LIKELY to remain loyal.

3.6 Relationship Strength Index (RSI)
──────────────────────────────────────

Composite metric combining 5 engagement dimensions:

RSI = (Active Status × 25) + (Products/4 × 25) + (Tenure/10 × 25)
      + (Credit Card × 15) + (Balance/Max Balance × 10)

Scale: 0-100

RSI Statistics:

Status    │ Mean RSI │ Risk Level
──────────┼──────────┼────────────────
Retained  │ 49.61    │ Healthy
Churned   │ 44.69    │ At-Risk

RSI Gap: 4.92 points (11% higher for retained customers)

Sticky Customer Profiles by RSI:

Profile               │ Count │ Churn Rate │ Avg RSI │ Avg Balance
──────────────────────┼───────┼────────────┼─────────┼─────────────
Sticky Elite          │ 1,565 │  11.82%    │  69.93  │  $65,295
Sticky Multi-Product  │ 1,023 │   6.35%    │  52.64  │  $34,372
Sticky Active         │ 2,012 │  17.59%    │  61.86  │ $102,316

Key Finding: Sticky Multi-Product customers have the LOWEST churn at 6.35%
despite having lower average balance — proving engagement beats wealth.


4. KEY PERFORMANCE INDICATORS
═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

KPI 1: ENGAGEMENT RETENTION RATIO
──────────────────────────────────
Metric: (1 - Active Churn) / (1 - Inactive Churn)
Result: 1.17x

Business Interpretation:
Active members are 17% more likely to retain than inactive members.
With 4,849 inactive customers, engagement activation is the top priority.

Actionable Insight:
Target: Activate inactive members through personalized campaigns
Estimated Impact: 320+ churn preventions per 50% activation rate


KPI 2: PRODUCT DEPTH INDEX
──────────────────────────
Metric: (1 - Multi-Product Churn) / (1 - Single-Product Churn)
Result: 1.21x

Business Interpretation:
Multi-product customers are 21% more likely to stay.
Optimal product count is 2 — beyond that churn rises sharply.

Actionable Insight:
Cross-sell 1 additional product to 5,084 single-product customers
Estimated Impact: Reduce churn from 27.71% to ~7.58%


KPI 3: HIGH-BALANCE DISENGAGEMENT RATE
───────────────────────────────────────
Metric: Churn rate of (Balance > 75th percentile AND Inactive)
Result: 30.47%

Business Interpretation:
Premium inactive customers churn at 1.50x the overall average.
$185.6M in AUM is at risk from 1,247 customers.

Actionable Insight:
Priority intervention for 1,247 at-risk premium customers
Estimated AUM protected: $185,625,527


KPI 4: CREDIT CARD STICKINESS SCORE
────────────────────────────────────
Metric: (1 - CC Churn) / (1 - No CC Churn)
Result: 1.01x

Business Interpretation:
Minimal retention difference between cardholders and non-cardholders.
Focus should shift to engagement and product depth rather than card push.


KPI 5: RELATIONSHIP STRENGTH INDEX
────────────────────────────────────
Metric: Composite 0-100 scale (Engagement + Products + Tenure + CC + Balance)
Result: 49.61 (retained) vs 44.69 (churned)
Gap: 4.92 points

Business Interpretation:
RSI provides an early warning signal for churn.
Customers with RSI < 40 are in the high-risk zone requiring intervention.

Actionable Insight:
Monitor RSI quarterly; trigger outreach for customers with RSI < 40
At-Risk Premium customers average RSI of 38.19 — below the intervention threshold

7. LIMITATIONS & FUTURE RESEARCH
═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

7.1 Study Limitations
─────────────────────
• Cross-sectional design — no temporal trends observed
• Limited to 3 geographies (France, Spain, Germany)
• No transaction-level behavioral data available
• No external macro factors (interest rates, competitor actions)
• Churn definition: simple binary exit — reasons not captured
• 3 and 4-product anomaly requires further investigation

7.2 Future Research Directions
──────────────────────────────
1. Time-Series Analysis
   └─ Track customer lifecycle transitions over time
   └─ Identify early churn warning windows (3-6 months before exit)

2. Product Interaction Analysis
   └─ Which specific 2-product combinations have lowest churn?
   └─ Why do 3-4 product customers churn at 83-100%?

3. Segmentation Refinement
   └─ Demographic × Behavioral clustering (K-means, DBSCAN)
   └─ Micro-segment targeted interventions

4. Predictive Modeling
   └─ Machine learning churn prediction (Random Forest, XGBoost)
   └─ Propensity scoring for cross-sell recommendations

5. Zero-Balance Investigation
   └─ Why do 36% of customers hold zero balance?
   └─ Are these account holders using other banks as primary?


8. CONCLUSION
═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

This analysis fundamentally reframes customer churn from a demographic perspective
to a behavioral and relationship-strength lens. Four critical findings emerge:

1. ENGAGEMENT MATTERS — BUT NOT AS DRAMATICALLY AS EXPECTED
   Active members retain at 85.73% vs inactive at 73.15% (1.17x ratio).
   With 4,849 inactive customers, engagement activation is the top priority.

2. 2 PRODUCTS IS THE SWEET SPOT
   2-product customers churn at only 7.58% vs 27.71% for single-product.
   But 3+ products causes churn to spike — over-selling must stop.

3. AT-RISK PREMIUM SEGMENT DEMANDS IMMEDIATE ACTION
   1,247 high-balance inactive customers hold $185.6M AUM.
   At 30.47% churn rate, ~$56.5M is at immediate risk of exit.

4. ZERO-BALANCE CUSTOMERS ARE AN UNTAPPED OPPORTUNITY
   3,617 customers (36%) hold zero balance — yet remain customers.
   These represent cross-sell and deposit-growth opportunities.

By implementing the five recommendations, the bank can:
├─ Protect $185.6M in at-risk AUM
├─ Reduce single-product churn by up to 20 percentage points
├─ Build a proactive RSI-based early warning system
└─ Transform from reactive to data-driven retention strategy

The evidence is clear: Customer retention is not about balance sheets — it is about
relationships, meaningful engagement, and optimal product integration.
